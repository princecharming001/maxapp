**assistant:** **mewing** is a tongue-posture technique where you place your tongue flat on the roof of your mouth (back third), seal your lips, and keep your teeth in light contact. the idea is that sustained correct tongue position trains your palate and supports facial structure over time. **active hold** is the deliberate 60-second engagement you do each morning at wake, where you consciously press your tongue up, seal your lips, tuck your chin, and hold it.

after that initial minute, you maintain the posture passively throughout the day without active effort. **passive maintenance** is keeping the tongue-on-palate position going all day after your morning active hold, without conscious muscle tension. it's the low-effort version that trains habit and postural memory. **midday reset** is a 30-second conscious check-in at the midpoint between wake and sleep, where you re-engage the tongue-up, lips-sealed, jaw-unclenched position to counteract slouching and screen posture. **night-set hold** is a final posture cue you do about 30 minutes before bed, reinforcing the tongue-on-palate position as you transition to sleep.

this trains your sleeping posture so the position carries through the night. **real talk**: for adults with closed growth plates, mewing produces marginal visible change on its own. the bigger moves are masseter training (jaw muscle thickening) and neck training (head posture), which shift perceived jawline sharpness way more than tongue posture alone.
