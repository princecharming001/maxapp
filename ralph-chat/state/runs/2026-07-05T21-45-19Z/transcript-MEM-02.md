## turn 6 ('what causes dandruff')
**user:** what causes dandruff
**assistant:** dandruff usually comes from one of three things: dry scalp (especially in winter or with harsh shampoos), malassezia yeast overgrowth (most common), or seborrheic dermatitis. the yeast feeds on scalp oil, so oily scalps like yours are more prone to it.

fix it with:

1. **anti-dandruff shampoo** — look for zinc pyrithione or ketoconazole (kills the yeast). use 2–3×/week, leave it on for a minute before rinsing.
2. **scalp massage** — 2–3 min daily with your fingertips to improve circulation and reduce buildup.
3. **moisturize after** — use a lightweight scalp oil or leave-in conditioner so you don't overcorrect into dryness.
4. **avoid hot water** — wash with lukewarm water; hot water strips oils and irritates the scalp.

if it doesn't clear in 2–3 weeks, it might be seborrheic dermatitis and you'd want a dermatologist check. but start with the yeast angle first since that's the most common culprit for oily scalps.

## turn 10 ("how do i know if i'm overtraining")
**user:** how do i know if i'm overtraining
**assistant:** overtraining shows up as: persistent fatigue even after rest days, strength plateauing or dropping, sleep getting worse, mood tanking, joint aches that don't go away, and elevated resting heart rate (5–10 bpm higher than baseline). if you're hitting 3 days a week with dumbbells, you're probably fine — that's moderate volume. but watch for those signals.

fix it by: taking a full rest day (zero activity) once a week, sleeping 7–9 hours consistently, eating enough calories to support your training, and deloading every 4–6 weeks (cut volume by 40–50% for one week). if fatigue sticks around for 2+ weeks, pull back for a week and reassess. recovery is where the gains happen, not in the gym.
