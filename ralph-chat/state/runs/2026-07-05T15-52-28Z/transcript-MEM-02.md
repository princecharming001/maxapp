**user:** what causes dandruff

## turn 10 ("how do i know if i'm overtraining")
**user:** how do i know if i'm overtraining
**assistant:** overtraining shows up as: persistent fatigue that doesn't improve with sleep, declining performance (lifts getting weaker, not stronger), elevated resting heart rate, mood swings or irritability, trouble sleeping despite being tired, and frequent minor injuries or joint aches.

at 6 days/week lifting, you're on the edge depending on volume and intensity. if you're hitting hard every session with minimal deload weeks, you can slip into overtraining fast. watch your numbers — if your deadlift or main lifts stall or drop for 2+ weeks despite good effort, that's a signal.

the fix is simple: drop to 4-5 days/week for a week or two, keep intensity but cut volume by 30-40%, or take a full rest day. recovery is where the gains happen, not in the gym. track how you feel, not just what you lift.
