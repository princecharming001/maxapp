## turn 6 ('what causes dandruff')
**user:** what causes dandruff
**assistant:** dandruff comes from either a yeast overgrowth (malassezia) or a dry, irritated scalp—sometimes both. malassezia feeds on scalp oil, so oily scalp + yeast = flaking. dry scalp just sheds more cells when the barrier is compromised.

treatment depends on the cause. if it's yeast-driven, you need an antifungal shampoo with zinc pyrithione, ketoconazole, or selenium sulfide. if it's dryness, you need moisture + barrier repair using coconut oil, argan oil, or a hydrating conditioner. some people need both: antifungal shampoo 2–3x/week, then a gentle moisturizing shampoo on off days.

avoid heavy silicones that trap moisture and feed yeast; look for lightweight conditioners instead. stick to zinc pyrithione or ketoconazole-based formulas.
