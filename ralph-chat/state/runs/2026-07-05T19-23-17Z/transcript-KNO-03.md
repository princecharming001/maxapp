**assistant:** mewing is tongue posture training. you put your tongue on the roof of your mouth (back third), seal your lips, and keep your teeth in light contact. the idea is that sustained palatal pressure + proper head posture shifts how your face reads over time. does it work for adults. not really, not on its own. your skull bones are fused after your mid-20s, so mewing won't reshape bone. what it can do is train postural awareness and reduce forward head posture, which makes your jawline look sharper by changing how your neck and jaw muscles sit. that's a real but marginal win. the bigger moves are masseter training (thickens jaw muscle visibly in 8-12 weeks) and neck work (keeps your head up and back so the jawline doesn't collapse into softness). if you're serious about jaw aesthetics, mewing is the foundation habit, but it's not the driver. body fat under 15% is the single biggest lever. the protocol is 3 quick resets a day: morning at wake (60s active hold, then passive), midday reset (30s conscious check), and night before bed. low friction, worth doing, but don't expect it to rebuild your face.
