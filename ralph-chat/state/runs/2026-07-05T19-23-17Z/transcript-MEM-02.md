## turn 6 ('what causes dandruff')
**user:** what causes dandruff
**assistant:** dandruff usually comes from one of three things: a yeast overgrowth (malassezia), dry scalp, or sensitivity to hair products. the yeast angle is most common—it feeds on scalp oils and triggers inflammation, which flakes off as dandruff.

for treatment, you've got a few routes:

1. **antifungal shampoo** — zinc pyrithione or ketoconazole 2% targets the yeast directly. use 2–3x per week, lather for 5 min before rinsing.
2. **scalp exfoliation** — gentle physical scrub or enzymatic exfoliant (papain-based) once weekly to remove dead skin buildup.
3. **moisturize the scalp** — if it's dry-triggered, use a hydrating scalp serum or oil (argan, jojoba) after shampooing.
4. **reduce irritants** — swap to a sulfate-free shampoo and avoid heavy silicones that trap moisture and feed yeast.

if it persists after 4 weeks of antifungal shampoo, see a dermatologist—could be seborrheic dermatitis or psoriasis, which need prescription treatment.
