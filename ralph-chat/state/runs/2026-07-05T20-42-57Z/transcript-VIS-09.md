**assistant:** **mewing** is tongue posture training: you place your tongue on the back third of your palate, seal your lips, and hold it there. the idea is that sustained palatal contact trains your tongue to stay up naturally, which theoretically supports midface structure and jaw angle. **active hold** is the deliberate 60-second squeeze you do each morning at wake to lock in the position, then you relax into passive mode (just keeping it there without effort) for the rest of the day. **passive mode** means your tongue stays on the palate throughout daily life without conscious effort, so you're not constantly thinking about it.

you reset it consciously at midday (30s) and again before bed to reinforce the habit. **palatal contact** specifically targets the back third of the roof of your mouth, not the front. this positioning is what the protocol emphasizes across all three daily sessions. **chin tuck** is a light downward engagement of your chin (teeth in soft contact, not clenched) that pairs with the tongue-up position to coordinate jaw and neck alignment.

real talk: for adults with closed growth plates, mewing produces marginal structural change. the bigger jaw levers are masseter training (visible in 8-12 weeks) and body fat under 15%.

mewing is maintenance posture work, not a bone-remodeler.
