## turn 6 ('what causes dandruff')
**user:** what causes dandruff
**assistant:** dandruff usually comes down to one of three things: a yeast called malassezia overgrowth (most common), dry scalp, or sensitivity to hair products. malassezia feeds on scalp oils, so it thrives in oily environments — which is why dandruff often gets worse in winter or with heavy product buildup.

treatment depends on the cause. if it's yeast-driven, you need an antifungal shampoo with zinc pyrithione, ketoconazole, or selenium sulfide. if it's dry scalp, switch to a gentler cleanser and use a hydrating conditioner. if it's product sensitivity, strip back to basics — simple shampoo, rinse well, no heavy leave-ins for a week and see if it clears.

use your chosen antifungal shampoo 2–3×/week until it clears, then dial back to maintenance (1×/week). zinc pyrithione and ketoconazole are your best bets for effective, gentle control.
