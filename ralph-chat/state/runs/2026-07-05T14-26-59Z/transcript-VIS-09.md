**assistant:** **mewing** is a tongue-posture technique where you place your tongue on the back third of your palate, seal your lips, and keep your teeth in light contact. the idea is that sustained proper tongue position trains your palate and supports facial structure over time, though for adults with closed growth plates the changes are marginal. **palate contact** means pressing your tongue flat against the roof of your mouth (specifically the back third, not the front).

this is the active part of the hold. **lip seal** means your lips stay closed throughout. this prevents mouth breathing and reinforces nasal breathing, which is part of the postural stack. **chin tuck** is a slight backward draw of your chin, keeping your head stacked over your neck instead of forward.

this prevents the "tech neck" slouch that softens your jawline. **active hold** is a deliberate 60-second contraction at wake, where you consciously press your tongue up and hold everything tight. after that, you switch to passive mode (same position, zero effort) for the rest of the day. **passive mode** means you maintain the tongue-on-palate position without muscular tension, just as a resting posture.

this is sustainable all day and trains habit. **midday reset** is a 30-second conscious check at the midpoint between wake and sleep, where you reset tongue, lips, and jaw alignment. it catches slouch creep from screen time or desk work.

the protocol runs 3 sessions daily (morning, midday, night) and stacks with chewing and neck work for real visible change in jaw definition. for adults, mewing alone produces minimal bone change, but combined with masseter training and low body fat, it's part of a working stack.
