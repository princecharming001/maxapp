**user:** how do i debloat my face
**assistant:** debloating depends on what's causing the puff. the main culprits are sleep position, sodium, dehydration, and inflammation.

**sleep position** — side or back sleeping keeps fluid from pooling in your face overnight. stomach sleeping pushes your face into the pillow and traps fluid. switch tonight if you're a stomach sleeper.

**sodium and hydration** — sounds backwards, but dehydration makes you retain water. drink 2.5–3l daily and dial back processed foods (chips, deli meat, sauces). high sodium makes you hold onto fluid, especially if you're not drinking enough.

**morning lymphatic drainage** — 30–60 seconds of upward strokes (jawline → temples → forehead), then drain downward behind your ears. do this right after waking while you're still a bit puffy. it moves fluid out and reduces puffiness within minutes.

**reduce inflammation** — if the puff is chronic, cut back on seed oils, sugar, and alcohol for a week and see if it improves. these drive systemic inflammation that shows up as facial bloat.

**cold water or ice** — splash your face with cold water or hold an ice roller on your cheeks and forehead for 30 seconds. vasoconstriction reduces swelling fast, lasts a couple hours.

if you're doing all this and still puffy, could be thyroid, hormonal, or a food sensitivity. that's worth checking with a doc.
