## turn 6 ('what causes dandruff')
**user:** what causes dandruff
**assistant:** dandruff usually comes from one of three things: a dry scalp (especially in winter or with hot water), fungal overgrowth (malassezia yeast), or sensitivity to sulfates in shampoo.

for your situation, focus on antifungal shampoos that work differently: zinc pyrithione or ketoconazole are both effective options without triggering your sensitivities. alternatively, switch to a sulfate-free, gentle shampoo if your scalp is primarily dry.

the protocol: use warm (not hot) water, massage your scalp for 2 minutes before rinsing, and let shampoo sit for 1–2 minutes so it actually works. if it's fungal, you might need ketoconazole 2% applied 2–3×/week for a few weeks, then drop to 1×/week maintenance. if it's dryness, a lightweight scalp oil (argan, jojoba) 2–3×/week helps. avoid tight hats and excessive heat styling, both of which trap moisture and feed fungal growth.
