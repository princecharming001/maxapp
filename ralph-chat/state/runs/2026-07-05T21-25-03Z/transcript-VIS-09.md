**assistant:** **mewing** is tongue posture training. you place your tongue on the back third of your palate, seal your lips, and hold it there passively throughout the day. the idea is that sustained palatal contact shifts how your face develops, but for adults with closed growth plates, the effect is marginal—it's more about maintaining posture and coordinating with the rest of the stack. **active hold** is the deliberate 60-second morning reset where you consciously press your tongue up, seal your lips, tuck your chin slightly, and keep your teeth in light contact. this primes your nervous system and sets the pattern for the rest of the day. **passive hold** is the background posture you maintain after the active hold ends. you're not thinking about it constantly, just keeping your tongue up and lips sealed while you work, eat, or move through your day. **midday reset** is a 30-second conscious check-in at the midpoint between wake and bed (roughly midday for you, around 15:00). you reset tongue position, unsclench your jaw, and realign your head over your neck. this counteracts slouching and screen-time drift. **night-set hold** happens 30 minutes before bed. you do a final conscious hold and coordinate it with sleep posture cues so you're not fighting tension all night. the real jaw gains come from **masseter training** (chewing hard gum, jawzrsize) and **neck training**, not mewing alone. mewing is the postural backbone that makes those work better.
