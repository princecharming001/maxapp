## turn 10 ("how do i know if i'm overtraining")
**user:** how do i know if i'm overtraining
**assistant:** overtraining shows up as: persistent fatigue even after rest days, strength plateaus or drops, elevated resting heart rate (5–10 bpm higher than baseline), mood crashes, sleep quality tanking, and increased injury risk. if you're hitting 5 days/week of deadlifts as a beginner, watch for these signs closely—beginners often push too hard too fast because the weight feels light.

the fix is simple: deload. drop volume by 40–50% for a week (lighter weight, fewer reps, same frequency), then ramp back up. if you're sleeping 8 hours, eating enough protein, and hydrating, you should recover fine. if fatigue persists even after a deload, dial back to 3–4 days/week and build slower.

log your workouts and how you feel after. if performance dips or you're dragging through sessions, that's your signal to back off.
