## turn 6 ('what causes dandruff')
**user:** what causes dandruff
**assistant:** dandruff usually comes from one of three things: a yeast overgrowth (malassezia), dry scalp, or sensitivity to hair products. the yeast angle is most common—it feeds on scalp oils and triggers flaking and irritation. dry scalp happens when you're washing too hot or too often, stripping natural oils. product sensitivity is less common but real if you're using heavy silicones or sulfates.

for treatment, go for a zinc pyrithione or ketoconazole shampoo (kills the yeast), or a gentle sulfate-free option paired with scalp massage to boost circulation. if it's dry scalp, a lightweight oil or scalp serum before bed helps. if you're already on a retinoid routine for skin, that can sometimes dry the scalp too—just keep your shampoo gentle and moisturizing.
