**assistant:** **mewing** is a tongue-posture technique where you place your tongue on the roof of your mouth (back third of the palate) with lips sealed and teeth in light contact, then hold it passively throughout the day. the idea is that sustained palatal contact trains your tongue to support midface structure and can shift how your jaw and face read over time. **morning active hold** is a 60-second deliberate engagement at wake where you actively press your tongue up, seal your lips, tuck your chin slightly, and lock the position before switching to passive mode for the rest of the day.

this primes your nervous system and sets the posture baseline. **passive mode** means you maintain tongue-on-palate contact throughout the day without active effort, just as a resting posture. it's the low-effort maintenance piece that stacks into normal life. **midday reset** is a conscious 30-second check at the midpoint between wake and sleep where you re-engage the position (tongue up, lips sealed, jaw unclenched, head over neck) to correct any drift from slouching or screen time. **night-set hold** is a deliberate posture cue 30 minutes before bed to reinforce tongue and head position during sleep, when postural habits lock in. **reality check for adults**: mewing alone produces marginal structural change in closed-suture faces.

the real leverage comes from pairing it with masseter training (chewing hard gum) and neck work, which actually build visible muscle and shift jaw definition in 8-12 weeks.
