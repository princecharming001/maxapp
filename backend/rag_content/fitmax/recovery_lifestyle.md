# Recovery & Lifestyle

Muscle isn't built in the gym. The gym creates the stimulus. Recovery does the building. Bad recovery → no progress, regardless of how hard you train.

## Why Recovery Matters

When recovery is bad you'll see:
- Weak workouts, dropping strength
- Bad sleep, broken nights
- Poor pumps and flat muscles
- Irritability, low motivation
- Constant lingering soreness
- Worse skin
- Plateau on the scale and in the mirror

If three or more of those are happening, your problem isn't training — it's recovery.

## Sleep — The Master Variable

Sleep affects every output:
- Muscle growth signaling (GH pulses, IGF-1)
- Hormones (test, cortisol, leptin)
- Mood and motivation
- Skin condition
- Appetite regulation
- Recovery between sessions
- How your face reads

Key sleep stats:
- **7–9 hours** is the evidence-based target for optimal recovery and hypertrophy
- **~70–80% of daily growth hormone (GH)** is released during slow-wave sleep (within the first 2 hours of sleep)
- **Sleep deprivation (< 6 hr/night)**: reduces testosterone by **10–15%** within a week; suppresses GH pulse amplitude
- **60-minute sleep wind-down**: cutting stimulation 60 min before bed measurably improves slow-wave sleep quality
- **Room temperature 65–68°F (18–20°C)**: linked to better deep-sleep architecture

Targets:
- 7–9 hours, consistent window (±30 min)
- Dark, cool (65–68°F / 18–20°C), quiet room
- Phone outside the bedroom or on do-not-disturb
- No alcohol within 4 h of bed
- Last meal 2–3 h before bed
- Caffeine cutoff before noon if you sleep poorly

## Overtraining Signs

- Strength dropping for 2+ consecutive sessions
- No motivation to train
- Bad sleep despite normal habits
- Constant joint aches
- No pump, no skin tightness during training
- Getting sick more than usual
- Mood crashes

The fix is not always more discipline. Often it's:
- Reducing junk volume (cut to 8–10 hard sets per muscle for 1 week)
- Eating more (especially carbs)
- Sleeping more
- Taking 1 extra full rest day for 1–2 weeks

## Recovery Habits That Compound

- 1–2 full rest days per week
- 2–3 L water/day; more if training hard or in heat
- Walking daily (8–10k steps) — boosts recovery, doesn't crush it like cardio
- Brief warmups before lifting (5 min general, 1 ramp set per main lift)
- Stretching only if mobility is genuinely limited (otherwise overrated)
- Consistent sleep schedule — even bedtime within ±30 min beats long weekends

## Stress Management

Chronic stress = chronic cortisol = blunted recovery + facial puffiness + worse skin + sleep disruption.

- Get sunlight in the first hour of waking
- Walk outside daily
- Limit news/scrolling that spikes anxiety
- Have at least one hobby that isn't optimizing your body

## Hygiene (often overlooked)

A built body with bad hygiene loses impact:
- Shower after every workout
- Use deodorant; reapply if you sweat through
- Clean clothes — gym shirts get bacterial fast
- Don't drown yourself in cologne — light spritz at the chest only
- Keep gym shoes and bag from getting funky (air them out, don't leave wet clothes inside)

## Key Takeaway

Train hard, recover hard, sleep harder. Most plateaus are recovery debt, not effort debt.
